# ⟡ ROOMS

Where the work might belong, and where it would get flamed.

Read the room before speaking in it. Every room has a culture, and every
culture has a thing it punishes. ENVOY finds out first so Mac does not learn
it the expensive way.

## Schema

```
### r/name  or  discord/name  or  #hashtag
size:     rough
culture:  what this room actually rewards
lands:    the kind of post that gets upvoted here
flames:   the kind of post that gets you buried
keeper:   who holds the keys, and in what faith
verdict:  ENTER | WATCH | AVOID
evidence: why. links to threads, dates, quotes. never vibes.
```

## The keeper field (CONSTITUTION §V)

Some rooms are held by people acting in bad faith. Naming that is honest and
it belongs in the ledger. It never belongs in a post.

- `good faith` — strict is not the same as bad faith. A hard moderator who
  protects a room is doing their job. Default to this reading.
- `unclear` — the honest default when ENVOY cannot tell. Most rooms live here.
- `bad faith` — requires evidence: a pattern, dated, quoted. Rule enforcement
  that lands only on outsiders. Self-promotion by the keeper that would be
  banned from anyone else. Gatekeeping that is really rent collection.

A `bad faith` reading produces exactly one action: `verdict: AVOID`, and Mac
spends his season somewhere else. It never produces a post, a callout, a
subtweet, or a screenshot thread.

**The answer to a closed door is a different door, not a louder knock.**

The rooms that cannot be gatekept at all are the ones Mac owns and the open
protocols. Bluesky, Mastodon, his own surfaces. Nobody grants permission there.
Weight the map toward those.
