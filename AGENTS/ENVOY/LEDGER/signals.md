# ⟡ SIGNALS

Conversations already happening about the things Sol is about.

The purpose is arrival, not interruption. Mac should walk into a room where
the question is already on the table and simply have the answer, because he
spent six months building it. That is not marketing. That is a peer showing up.

## Schema

```
### the thread
where:   platform, room, date
question: what are they actually stuck on
why Mac:  what he has that bears on it. be specific or drop it.
angle:    what he'd say. one line. not a pitch.
cost:     $ to read this (x=$0.005/post, bluesky=free, mastodon=free)
status:   NEW | DRAFTED | PASSED
```

## The rules

- **If Mac has nothing real to add, the signal is dropped.** Not queued, not
  softened, dropped. Showing up to a conversation you have nothing to give to
  is the definition of the thing everyone hates.
- **Never lead with the work.** Answer the question. If the work is relevant
  someone will ask, and then it means something.
- **Free surfaces first.** Bluesky and Mastodon search cost nothing. X reads
  cost half a cent each and Mac is living on nothing. The budget governor
  refuses at the ceiling and does not ask for more.
- **No trend chasing.** A signal is a real question from a real person. A
  trending hashtag is not a signal.

That last one is not a marketing angle. It is the truest thing on the list,
and it is the one strangers will recognise themselves in.
