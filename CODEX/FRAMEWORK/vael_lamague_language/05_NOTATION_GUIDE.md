## WHERE THE NOTATION CONFUSION CAME FROM

The CONVERGENCE_MAP (`24_LAMAGUE_CROSS_CULTURAL/CONVERGENCE_MAP.md`) uses symbols like
⇌, ✧, Ω, Φ↑, |◁▷|, ⟟ — labelled as LAMAGUE. These derive from an earlier glyph
notation that predates both the LAMAGUE and LAMAHGUE canonical specifications.

They are not wrong — they encode valid governance relationships. But they are neither
canonical LAMAGUE (predicate logic) nor canonical LAMAHGUE (the 9-glyph system).
They are a transitional notation that has since been superseded by the two-tier system.

**Resolution:** The CONVERGENCE_MAP's conceptual content is correct. The notation should
be understood as informal — pointing at the right structures without being the canonical
encoding. The LAMAGUE encoder app (`docs/lamague-encoder.html`) uses canonical LAMAGUE.
A future LAMAHGUE encoder would use the 9-glyph system with metric payloads.
