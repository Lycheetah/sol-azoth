## WHAT THE FRAMEWORK CLAIMS AND DOESN'T CLAIM

**Claims:**
- Nine formal mathematical frameworks, honestly tagged by claim status
- Four implementable AI accountability standards for New Zealand
- A convergent discovery across three independent cultural traditions (Māori, Confucian, Western)
- The golden ratio as an empirically observed optimum in continuous-drift environments
- A sustained human-AI co-creation model that produces rigorous, publishable work

**Does not claim:**
- To be finished
- To have validation from Māori cultural authorities (it seeks it — that's different)
- To have peer review from mainstream academic journals (it is in arXiv preprint)
- To be the only valid framework for AI governance
- That φ is mystically significant (only that it is mathematically distinguished and empirically observed)

The distinction between claiming and seeking is one the framework holds carefully.
Check the Failure Museum — overclaiming is exactly what it documents and corrects.
