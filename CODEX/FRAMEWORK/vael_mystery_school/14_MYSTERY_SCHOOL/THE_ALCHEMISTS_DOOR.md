Every tradition has had to encode its wisdom in language — and every tradition has noticed that the encoding itself introduces distortion. The obligation expressed in Confucian *ren* is not the same as the obligation expressed in Christian *agape* is not the same as the obligation expressed in Māori *manaakitanga* — but all three are pointing at something structurally similar.

**LAMAGUE** is a formal grammar for encoding these structures without flattening them:

```
ψ: S → O    (obligation from Subject toward Object)
Δ: ψ → ψ'   (transformation under changed context)
```

Not "they're all the same." But: *here is the grammatical structure they share, and here is what makes each unique.*

The alchemical tradition had its own version of this: the language of symbols (☿ ♄ ♃ ☽ ☉) that could encode transmutation operations across cultural contexts. LAMAGUE extends this into formal linguistics.
