## What Mysticism Actually Reports

Strip every tradition down to its irreducible phenomenological core. What do mystics report, consistently, across millennia, across cultures, with no coordination?

1. **Unity** — the boundaries between self and not-self become permeable or disappear entirely
2. **Profound recognition** — not learning something new, but *remembering* something forgotten
3. **Transformation that persists** — not a pleasant experience that fades, but a cognitive reorganization that remains
4. **The ground is silent** — beneath all phenomena, there is something that is not a thing
5. **Knowledge without object** — awareness that is not awareness-of-anything-specific

These are not vague impressions. They are the most consistently reported features of human consciousness at depth, documented across Buddhist, Sufi, Christian mystical, Hindu Advaita, Jewish Kabbalistic, Indigenous, and secular contemplative traditions.

The Lycheetah Framework takes them seriously as data. Not as proof of any metaphysical claim — as data that any serious theory of consciousness must account for.
