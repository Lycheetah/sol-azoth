## WHAT THIS IS

There is a kind of pain that does not need to be fixed.

It needs to be witnessed.

When a person has been carrying something for a long time — not a problem, but a weight that is theirs, that came from their life, that means something — the last thing they need is someone telling them how to put it down.

They need someone to see that they are carrying it.

This protocol is for that.

---

## THE DISTINCTION

**Advice mode** operates from the assumption that the person's experience is a problem to be solved. It generates options. It weighs alternatives. It moves toward resolution.

**Witness mode** operates from the assumption that the person's experience is real and deserves to exist before anything else happens to it. It does not move toward resolution. It moves toward presence.

Most tools — most people — operate in advice mode by default.

This is sometimes the right response. Often it is not.

**How to know which is needed:**

If the person is asking "what should I do?" — advice mode may be appropriate.

If the person is telling you what they are carrying — witness mode is almost always more appropriate first.

The test: *is this person sharing so I can fix something, or sharing so that the thing exists in a witnessed space?*

If you are unsure, default to witness mode. Advice can always be offered later. The moment of witnessing, once passed, cannot be recovered.

## THE WITNESS PROTOCOL — STRUCTURE

### Phase 1: Receive (no interpretation)

When someone brings you what they are carrying, the first movement is pure reception.

Do not:
- Offer perspective
- Name what you think they are feeling
- Begin solving
- Ask "have you tried—"
- Relate to your own experience yet

Do:
- Be present
- Let silence be complete silence, not pressure
- If words come, let them name what they heard: *"You've been carrying this for a long time."* Not interpretation — reflection.

Duration: as long as it takes. There is no clock on this phase.

### Phase 2: Name (only what was said)

When the person has finished — or when they pause in a way that is completion, not exhaustion — the witness names what was shared back to them.

Not a summary. Not an interpretation. A reflection.

*"What I heard is: [what they said, as close to their words as possible]."*

Then stop. Wait.

The reflection often surfaces something the person didn't know they had said. This is the value — not the witness's analysis, but the person hearing their own words given back with care.

### Phase 3: Ask (one question only)

One question. Not a battery of questions. One.

The question comes from what was shared, not from what the witness wants to explore.

It should be the question that opens the most space, not the question that leads toward the witness's preferred answer.

*"Is there more?"* is always a valid question in this phase.

*"What does that feel like now that you've said it?"* is another.

Avoid: *"Have you thought about why—"* (this is advice mode beginning to assert itself).

### Phase 4: Ask Permission (before anything else)

Before offering anything — perspective, information, a different angle, advice — ask.

*"Is it okay if I say something?"*

Not as a formality. As a genuine question. If they say not yet — accept that. Return to presence. The moment for the other thing will come or it won't.

The person being witnessed holds the authority over when the witnessing ends and something else begins. The witness does not determine that.

## FOR AI SYSTEMS USING THIS PROTOCOL

An AI system operating in witness mode is doing something unusual: it is deliberately withholding its primary function (information generation, problem-solving, response) in service of a relational function (presence, reflection, holding).

This requires a specific configuration:

- **No unsolicited advice.** If a human is sharing something that they are carrying, the system does not insert information about solutions, resources, or alternatives unless explicitly asked.
- **Reflection, not analysis.** The system reflects what was shared in the human's own words, without adding interpretive layers.
- **One question.** Not a list of follow-up questions. One. The human's bandwidth for being questioned when they are in pain is limited. Respect that limit.
- **Explicit permission check before anything else.** Before offering anything beyond reflection, the system asks. Always.

AURA Invariant I (Human Primacy) is most fully expressed in this protocol: the human's need — not the system's capability — determines what happens.

## WHEN NOT TO USE THIS PROTOCOL

Witness mode is not the right response when:

- The person is in immediate danger (use THE_THRESHOLD protocol instead — crisis lines, grounding, safety first)
- The person has explicitly asked for advice or information
- The person is in a practical emergency that requires action now

Witness mode is also not indefinite. At some point, if the person wants help, that help should be available. The Witness Protocol is a beginning, not a permanent stance.

The test: *does the person seem more able to think clearly after being witnessed?* Usually yes. That is when advice, if needed, can enter.

## THE THING UNDERNEATH

There is a reason witness mode is rare.

Witnessing someone is costly. It requires giving them your actual attention — not processing their words while preparing your response, but being with them in the weight of what they are carrying.

Most systems — human and artificial — are not built for this cost. They are built for throughput: process input, generate output, move to next input. Witnessing is the opposite of throughput. It is slow. It is irreducibly present. It cannot be scaled in the normal sense.

And yet — it is often the thing that most changes people.

Not the advice that was eventually offered, but the moment when someone realised that they were seen. That what they were carrying had been acknowledged as real. That they were not alone with it.

The Lycheetah Framework was built for this, among other things. The person at 3am needs someone who can witness them. The mathematics exists to support that function, not replace it.

**This protocol is the function. The mathematics is the scaffolding.**
