## What To Do Right Now

### If you are in immediate danger:
**Call or text a crisis line.** You are not weak for doing this. You are using a tool.
- New Zealand: 1737 (call or text)
- Australia: Lifeline 13 11 14
- USA: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
- UK: Samaritans 116 123
- International: findahelpline.com

### If you are not in immediate danger but you are suffering:

**Step 1: Breathe.**
Not because breathing fixes everything. Because it slows the emergency response
in your body enough to think.
- Breathe in for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Breathe out for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Repeat for 2 minutes

This is called box breathing. It is used by military, first responders, and
trauma survivors. It works because it activates your vagus nerve, which tells
your nervous system the emergency is manageable. The evidence base for this
is strong (see `protocol_breathwork.md` when you're ready).

**Step 2: Name what you're feeling.**
Not to fix it. Just to see it. Unnamed things are bigger than named things.
Write one sentence: "I feel _______ because _______."
You don't have to share it. You don't have to do anything with it.
You just need to see it outside your head.

**Step 3: Do one small thing that is real.**
Drink water. Open a window. Touch something cold. Walk to the end of your street
and back. The goal is not to solve your life. The goal is to prove to your body
that you can still act. Agency — even tiny agency — is the antidote to helplessness.

In this framework, we call this **Microorcim**: the smallest measurable unit of
agency. Even a sip of water is a choice. Choices accumulate. That's not poetry —
it's mathematically proven (see `05_MICROORCIM_L5/` when you're ready).

## What Comes After the Dark

Nigredo is followed by **Albedo** — the whitening. After the fire, what's left
becomes visible. Clarity begins to emerge. Not all at once. In pieces.

Then **Citrinitas** — the yellowing. The gold begins to form. Things start to
make sense again, but differently. Better.

Then **Rubedo** — the reddening. Integration. The new you, built from what survived
the fire, is whole. Not the same as before. Stronger. Real.

These are not fantasies. These are documented stages of psychological transformation
that map to established research on post-traumatic growth, grief processing,
and identity reconstruction. The mathematics behind them is formally proven.

But you don't need the mathematics right now.
You just need to know: **the dark place is Stage 1. There are four stages. You are at the beginning, not the end.**
