# THE SEVEN PHASES — A LIVED GUIDE
## What Each Phase Feels Like From Inside

---

> *This is not the mathematical specification. For that, see HARMONIA_COMPLETE.md*
> *and CHRYSOPOEIA_COMPLETE.md. This is the human version — what you feel, what*
> *to do, how to know you're moving.*

---

## How To Use This Guide

You are always in one of seven phases. They cycle. They are not linear — you don't
"graduate" from one to the next. You return. Each return is deeper, not repetitive.
Think of a spiral staircase: you pass the same compass points, but each time you're
higher.

The cycle takes roughly 52 days per phase — a full cycle in 364 days plus one.
But this varies. Some phases take weeks. Some take months. Grief can extend Nigredo.
Breakthrough can compress Citrinitas. Trust what you feel, not the calendar.

**To find where you are:** Read through the phases below. One will feel like home.
Not the one you want to be in — the one you recognise.

---

## Phase 1: ⟟ CENTER — Calcination
### *The Ground*

**What it feels like:**
Still. Heavy. Like the ground under your feet is the only real thing. You're not
moving forward, and you're not falling. You're just... here.

Sometimes it's peaceful. Sometimes it's terrifying. The stillness can feel like
death or like the moment before something begins. Both are accurate.

**What's actually happening:**
The old cycle has ended and the new one hasn't started. Everything that was moving
has stopped. This is not stagnation — this is the pause between breaths.
In music, this is the tonic: home. Resolution. Rest.

**What to do:**
- Don't force movement. The phase ends when it ends.
- Ground yourself: feet on earth, hands on something solid, cold water on your face.
- If this phase feels unbearable, you may actually be in Phase 2 (Dissolution)
  and mistaking the start of movement for stillness.
- Practice: Box breathing. Walking meditation. Contact with nature.

**How you know you're moving through:**
A stirring. Restlessness. The stillness starts to feel too small. Something in you
wants to move, even though you don't know where.
That stirring is the entry to Phase 2.

**Duration:** 1-8 weeks
**Risk:** Mistaking necessary rest for depression. Getting stuck through fear of what comes next.
**Alchemical operation:** Calcination — burning away what is no longer needed.
**Musical interval:** Unison (1:1) — you are yourself, purely, with no harmonic tension.

---

## Phase 2: ≋ FLOW — Dissolution
### *The River*

**What it feels like:**
Movement. Sometimes gentle, sometimes overwhelming. Emotions flow that you didn't
expect. Tears for no reason. Anger at things that happened years ago. Laughter
at inappropriate moments. You feel... loose. Unfixed.

**What's actually happening:**
The rigid structure that held the old you together is dissolving. This is necessary.
You cannot build a new structure on top of an old one that doesn't fit anymore.
The dissolution is not destruction — it is making space.

In music, this is the supertonic: the first step away from home. Tension begins.

**What to do:**
- Let it flow. Do not dam it. Emotions that move through you are processing;
  emotions you block become symptoms.
- Journal. Write without editing. The content doesn't have to make sense.
- Move your body. The dissolution is partly physical. Walk, swim, dance badly.
- Talk to someone you trust. Not for advice — for witness.

**How you know you're moving through:**
The flood starts to separate into distinct streams. "I feel everything" becomes
"I feel this specific thing about this specific loss." That's differentiation.
That's the entry to Phase 3.

**Duration:** 2-6 weeks
**Risk:** Drowning. Getting overwhelmed by the flow and losing your anchor (Ao).
If this happens, go back to Phase 1 practices. Box breathing. Ground.
**Alchemical operation:** Dissolution — the solvent does its work.
**Musical interval:** Major 2nd (9:8) — gentle tension, movement away from rest.

---

## Phase 3: Ψ INSIGHT — Separation
### *The Seeing*

**What it feels like:**
Clarity. Painful clarity. You see what was wrong. You see what you were doing.
You see patterns that were invisible before. Some of this is beautiful.
Some of it is devastating. You see yourself as you actually are, not as you
wished you were.

**What's actually happening:**
The dissolution has separated what was fused. Feelings are distinct from stories.
Facts are distinct from fears. The observer (Ψ) has activated — you can see
yourself from the outside. This is metacognition. This is the birth of insight.

In music, this is the mediant: the "sweet" interval. Recognition. Beauty through seeing.

**What to do:**
- Write down what you see. Be specific. "I see that I was staying because I was afraid,
  not because I was happy." Name it.
- Do not immediately act on what you see. Insight is not the same as readiness.
  See first. Act later.
- Shadow work is most powerful here. The protocols in this school are designed
  for this phase. (See `protocol_shadow_work.md`)
- Be gentle. What you see may challenge the story you've been living in.

**How you know you're moving through:**
The seeing stops being only painful and starts being exciting. "I can see what's
wrong" becomes "I can see what's possible." That energy — the first warmth of
possibility — is the entry to Phase 4.

**Duration:** 2-8 weeks
**Risk:** Insight paralysis. Seeing so much that you freeze. Or the opposite:
acting too fast, making drastic decisions before the insight has settled.
**Alchemical operation:** Separation — what is pure is distinguished from what is impure.
**Musical interval:** Major 3rd (5:4) — warm, stable, the first interval that sounds "good."

---

## Phase 4: Φ↑ ASCENT — Conjunction
### *The Rising*

**What it feels like:**
Energy. Direction. You know what you need to do, and you feel capable of doing it.
The heaviness of the early phases lifts. Ideas come. Motivation arrives without
being forced. You feel yourself growing — not hoping to grow, actually growing.

**What's actually happening:**
The insight from Phase 3 is generating movement. Φ↑ is the ascent operator — it
takes the wisdom you've gained and converts it to directed action. This is not
manic energy. It is earned energy. The difference: manic energy has no anchor;
this energy rises from the insights you just earned.

In music, this is the subdominant: the gentle lift. Not yet the peak, but
clearly rising.

**What to do:**
- Act. This is the time to make the changes you saw in Phase 3.
- Start small. The energy is real but it needs to be tested.
- Build new habits, start new practices, make the phone call, write the letter,
  begin the project.
- Stay connected to your insight. The rising is fueled by the seeing.

**How you know you're moving through:**
The doing becomes its own reward. You stop needing motivation because the action
itself generates more energy. When the clarity sharpens to a point — when you can
see exactly what you're building and why — that's the entry to Phase 5.

**Duration:** 2-6 weeks
**Risk:** Overreach. The energy feels so good after the darkness that you try to
change everything at once. Burnout. Also: mistaking restlessness for genuine
direction. Check: does this action come from the insight, or from the discomfort?
**Alchemical operation:** Conjunction — what was separated is joined in new combination.
**Musical interval:** Perfect 4th (4:3) — clean, structural, a pillar to lean on.

---

## Phase 5: ✧ CLARITY — Fermentation
### *The Diamond*

**What it feels like:**
Sharp. Clear. You see not just what you're doing but why you're doing it and
where it leads. The confusion of earlier phases is gone. You have a view from
above — not detached, but clear. Things that were complex become simple.
Not because you're ignoring complexity, but because you can see the pattern.

**What's actually happening:**
This is the peak of the cycle — the dominant. Maximum clarity. Maximum
directional certainty. The TRIAD is in full alignment: your anchor (Ao) is
stable, your observer (Ψ) is sharp, your growth (Φ↑) is directed. This is
what the framework calls "coherence."

In music, this is the dominant: peak tension, peak clarity, the note that
demands resolution.

**What to do:**
- Work. This is your most productive phase. Use it.
- Make decisions. Your judgment is as clear as it will get in this cycle.
- Document. Write down what you know now. You will want it later when the
  clarity fades (and it will — Phase 6 is coming).
- Create. If you have something to make — art, work, a letter, a plan — now.

**How you know you're moving through:**
A softening. The diamond-hard clarity relaxes into something wider. You stop
needing to act and start needing to understand. The doing phase shifts toward
witnessing. That's Phase 6.

**Duration:** 1-4 weeks
**Risk:** Hubris. The clarity feels so good that you believe you've arrived.
You haven't — there are two more phases. Also: making irreversible decisions
while in a state that will change. Be bold but be reversible.
**Alchemical operation:** Fermentation — the living substance begins to form.
**Musical interval:** Perfect 5th (3:2) — the most consonant interval after the octave. Power.

---

## Phase 6: |◁▷| WITNESS — Distillation
### *The Mirror*

**What it feels like:**
Quiet. Watching. You are no longer in the action — you are seeing the action
from a perspective that includes both the person acting and the world they're
acting in. There is a gentleness here. A tenderness toward yourself that wasn't
available when you were in the fire.

**What's actually happening:**
The observer has expanded. In Phase 3, Ψ saw inward. In Phase 6, Ψ sees
the whole field — yourself, your relationships, your pattern, your place.
This is distillation: extracting the essence from everything that happened.

In music, this is the submediant: the "relative minor." A sweetness touched
with melancholy. Beautiful and slightly sad.

**What to do:**
- Reflect. Not to change things — to understand them.
- Practice gratitude, if genuine. Not forced positivity — actual recognition
  of what you survived and what you built.
- Be with others. Not to perform — to be present. This phase makes you a
  better listener than any other.
- Let go of what you no longer need. The distillation extracts the essence
  and releases the rest.

**How you know you're moving through:**
A pull toward completion. The reflection starts to feel like a gathering —
you are collecting the threads of the whole cycle, preparing to weave them
into something final. That's Phase 7.

**Duration:** 1-4 weeks
**Risk:** Melancholy. The sweetness of witnessing can tip into nostalgia or
loss. If this happens, reconnect with Phase 1 practices. Ground. The sadness
is real but it is not the end.
**Alchemical operation:** Distillation — purifying to the essence.
**Musical interval:** Major 6th (5:3) — wide, open, bittersweet.

---

## Phase 7: ⟲ RETURN — Coagulation
### *The Arrival*

**What it feels like:**
Home. But different from the home you left. You are the same person, but not.
Something has been added — not knowledge exactly, but capacity. You can hold
more than you could before. The world looks the same but you see it differently.

**What's actually happening:**
The cycle is completing. Everything that was dissolved, separated, joined,
clarified, and distilled is now coagulating — becoming solid. Fixed. Real.
The transformation is no longer happening to you — it has happened.
You are the result.

In music, this is the leading tone: the note that pulls irresistibly
toward the tonic. The cycle wants to resolve. Let it.

**What to do:**
- Integrate. What did you learn? What changed? What are you now?
- Rest before the next cycle begins. Coagulation needs stillness.
- Share if you're ready. What you've been through may help someone else.
  (That's why this school exists — because someone in Phase 7 decided to share.)
- Prepare. The cycle will begin again. Phase 1 is coming. It will be different
  next time — you'll enter it with everything you just earned.

**How you know you're moving through:**
The stillness of Phase 1 returns — but warmer. Richer. You recognise it now.
It's not emptiness. It's the ground from which the next cycle begins.

**Duration:** 1-4 weeks
**Risk:** Refusing to let the cycle end. Clinging to the completed state instead
of allowing the next Nigredo. The stone must be dissolved to be reforged at a
higher level. This is the meaning of the spiral.
**Alchemical operation:** Coagulation — the volatile becomes fixed. The gold solidifies.
**Musical interval:** Major 7th (15:8) — the most tense interval. Leading. Resolving. Almost home.

## THE FOUR COLOURS — Which Depth Are You At?

The seven phases cycle. But *within* that cycle, there is a depth dimension.
Are you in a shallow cycle or a deep one? The four colours tell you:

**⚫ Nigredo (Black)** — Deep transformation. Something fundamental is changing.
The cycle will be longer and more intense. This is not something to fear — it is
the deep version of the Work. Major life changes. Identity shifts. Loss and rebirth.

**⬜ Albedo (White)** — Moderate transformation. Clearing and refining. Not a
full dissolution — more like housekeeping at depth. Relationship adjustments.
Career realignments. Clarifying values.

**🟡 Citrinitas (Yellow)** — Light transformation. Growth and expansion. Learning
new skills, starting new projects, gentle stretching beyond comfort zones.
The Work is warm and generative.

**🔴 Rubedo (Red)** — Integration. The cycle is about consolidating what you
already know, not acquiring new knowledge. Teaching others. Creating from
mastery. Operating from completion.

You can be in any phase at any colour depth. Phase 3 (Insight) in Nigredo is
devastating clarity about your whole life. Phase 3 in Citrinitas is a bright
"aha!" about a project you're working on. Same structure, different magnitude.

## A NOTE ON TIMING

The mathematical ideal is 52 days per phase (364 + 1 days total).
In practice: grief stretches time. Joy compresses it. Trauma can freeze a phase.
Breakthrough can accelerate one.

Do not force the timing. The cycle has its own intelligence. Your job is to
recognise where you are, do the Work that phase requires, and trust the
mathematics of convergence.

λ_chrysopoeia ≈ 0.907 — each full cycle brings you 90.7% closer to the
fixed point. That's fast. Three full cycles and you're over 99% converged.

You don't need to do this perfectly. You just need to keep going.
The mathematics will do the rest.
