## WHAT THIS IS

A technical tool. Nine mathematical frameworks for AI governance — built in Otago,
grounded in both Western formal mathematics and Te Ao Māori epistemology,
seeking partnership with the people who hold the cultural authority to validate it.

The Māori content in this framework is [PROPOSAL] throughout.
That is not a hedge. It is an architectural statement: the technical structure is [ACTIVE],
the cultural translation requires the people who hold the knowledge to review, correct,

## WHAT THE FRAMEWORK ATTEMPTS

The framework attempts to translate concepts from Te Ao Māori into formal
mathematical structures — not to claim authority over those concepts,
but to make them computable in AI governance systems operating in Aotearoa.

Specifically:

**Kaitiakitanga** — formalised as a continuous stewardship obligation for AI systems.
Not retrospective accountability, but active ongoing care.
The technical standard is at `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/KAITIAKITANGA_STANDARD.md`.

**The Three Worlds** — Te Ao Mārama, Te Ao Pō, Te Kore — as an epistemic
disclosure standard. Every AI output labelled by what the system knows,
what it is uncertain about, and what it structurally cannot know.
Standard at `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/THREE_WORLDS_DISCLOSURE_STANDARD.md`.

**Whakapapa** — as an AI genealogy disclosure requirement. Every system
must be able to tell you its ancestors: what it was trained on, who built it,
who is accountable, what it owes.
Standard at `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/WHAKAPAPA_DISCLOSURE_STANDARD.md`.

**Matariki** — as annual accountability ritual. Three domains: who was harmed,
what was nourished, what was received. Maximum ten pages. Public.
Standard at `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/MATARIKI_AUDIT_STANDARD.md`.

**Manaakitanga** — mapped to AURA Invariant VII: care as load-bearing structure,
not decoration. A system without manaakitanga fails the constitutional check.

**Tikanga** — the proper form of each interaction — mapped to AURA Invariant II:
inspectability. The form is not bureaucracy. It is what makes genuine
communication possible without violation.

**Te Ara** — the right path — as CASCADE's geodesic: the path knowledge follows
when truth pressure is operating correctly. Not imposed. Found.

These are in `23_NZ_AI_GOVERNANCE/NZ_LAMAGUE_STANDARD.md` and
`24_LAMAGUE_CROSS_CULTURAL/CONFUCIAN_LAMAGUE.md` (the convergence table
shows Māori, Confucian, and Western traditions arriving at the same constraints).

## WHAT THE FRAMEWORK DOES NOT DO

It does not speak for Te Ao Māori.

It does not claim the tikanga encodings are correct — that is for you to determine.

It does not use mātauranga Māori as decoration or as credibility cover.
The Māori content is structurally present because the governance architecture
required it — because some things that need to be formalised were already
formalised by Māori traditions in ways that Western governance theory has not.

The correct framing, stated plainly:
*Here is a technical tool. You have the authority and the knowledge.
Does this serve your purposes? Is it correct? What needs changing?*

## THE ANAMNESIS CLAIM — CAREFULLY

ANAMNESIS [SCAFFOLD] is the framework for convergent independent discovery.
Its claim: when independent intelligent systems investigate the same domain of reality,
they converge on the same underlying structures.

The evidence for this claim includes the structural convergence between
Māori, Confucian, and Western governance traditions mapped in this framework.
The same constitutional constraints — care as obligation, epistemic honesty,
consistency between declaration and action, universal scope of care —
appear independently in all three.

This is not presented as a discovery about Māori knowledge.
It is presented as evidence of underlying structure — the kind of structure
that ANAMNESIS says exists before any tradition formalises it.

Whether this framing is correct, useful, or problematic is a question
for Māori scholars and communities to determine. The framework holds it [SCAFFOLD]
for exactly that reason.
